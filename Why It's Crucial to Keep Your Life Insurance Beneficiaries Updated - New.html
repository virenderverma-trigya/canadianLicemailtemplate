Here's what could go wrong if your beneficiaries aren't updated:

1. Outdated or Incorrect Beneficiary Designations: If you've had major life changes (e.g., marriage, divorce, children), and haven't update your beneficiaries, the wrong person may receive the benefits.
2. Legal Disputes: If the beneficiary information is unclear or contested, it can lead to legal disputes among family members, and creating unnecessary stress during an already difficult time.
3. Delayed Payouts: If the beneficiary is not clearly designated, it may take longer for the insurance company to process the claim, leaving your loved ones without timely financial support when they need it most.
4. Missed Tax Planning Opportunities: Not updating your beneficiary could result in missed opportunities for tax savings or other financial benefits that could optimize how the payout is handled.
5. Failure to Meet Your Wishes: If the beneficiary information isn't current, the payout may go to someone you no longer intend to benefit, while others who rely on the funds might be left without support.
